# Excel Tips from the Support Team

You can enhance your Retail Express data management efficiency with Excel integration best practices, helping your retail operations handle bulk data operations and reporting effectively. This capability provides expert guidance on using Excel with Retail Express for imports, exports, and analysis, which helps optimize data handling workflows and reduce time spent on repetitive data tasks.

Setting up efficient Excel integration workflows enables your retail operations to manage large-scale data operations smoothly, helping optimize bulk updates and reporting processes. This helps retail businesses leverage familiar spreadsheet tools to enhance Retail Express functionality while maintaining data accuracy across large-scale operations.

The majority of reports available in Retail Express are available for export to Excel, giving you even greater reporting power! Using Excel you can manipulate the data to easily find more information, or create your own reports.

## Using Filters to find data in large spreadsheets

When reviewing a large volume of exported data it can sometimes be difficult to find what you are looking for. To assist with searching, filtering, and even grouping of information, the Filter tool can be very useful.

As an example we will use a Mass Download file, and search the Short Description column for all products with the word ?Water? in them in order to increase their price by 20%.

1. Navigate to: **Inventory > Mass Download**
2. Enter filter options as required
3. Click **Export to Excel**
4. Open the report in Excel
5. Click **Enable Editing**
6. Select the correct **Outlet** using the tabs at the bottom of your Mass Download spreadsheet
7. Click the** header of column (D)** so that the column is highlighted
  ![mceclip1 (39).png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000pox0PEAQ.jpg)
8. In the toolbar ribbon click **Sort & Filter** (in the Editing section)
  ![mceclip4 (12).png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000pox3SEAQ.jpg)
9. Click **Filter**
  ![mceclip2 (25).png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000pox3XEAQ.jpg)
10. At the top of the column a drop-down arrow will be displayed (just under the column heading)
  ![mceclip5 (6).png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000pox3cEAA.jpg)
11. **Click the arrow** to display the Filter menu
12. Select **Text Filters** and **Contains**
  ![mceclip6 (2).png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000pox3rEAA.jpg)
13. Enter the word to search for e.g. Water
14. Click **OK**
  ![mceclip7.png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000pox3wEAA.jpg)
15. Edit the product fields as required e.g. POS Price

> **Note:** The Filter options also contains other formulas as well as the ability to tick only specific products. Try playing around with these settings to see how they might assist you with finding data e.g. "Begins With", "Ends with". You can even try filtering with more than one column by selecting all the data in your spreadsheet (not just one column)

> **Note:** For more information refer to the Inventory - Using Mass Upload/Mass Download to create & edit products article.

## Use Conditional Formatting to find duplicate SKUs

Conditional Formatting gives you the ability to highlight data in the spreadsheet based on particular rules e.g. cells that contain specific values, or if more than one cell exists with matching data. This makes it perfect to use when creating products.

For example, if you're using the Mass Upload spreadsheet to create new products, you can use Conditional Formatting to highlight duplicate SKUs.

> **Note:** You can enable Conditional Formatting for the column before entering your products, so duplicates are highlighted as you create them.

- In the toolbar ribbon at the top of your screen click on the **Sort & Filter** button
- Click the **Filter** option again
- The filter will turn off (all rows in the spreadsheet will be displayed
- Save your spread sheet
- Upload the file in Retail Express to update the data
