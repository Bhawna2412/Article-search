# Restart Retail Express Hardware Utility Service

You can restart the **Retail Express** hardware utility service to resolve hardware connectivity issues, restoring communication with receipt printers, EFTPOS terminals, and other peripherals. This helps optimize your retail operations by providing quick resolution for hardware communication problems, minimizing POS downtime, and maintaining smooth transaction processing.

If you encounter any issues with Label Printing, or you're instructed by the support team, you may find that restarting the Retail Express Hardware Utility Service can resolve the issue.

The Utility can be restarted either from the Windows Services app, or directly within the Utility itself.

Please kindly follow the 2 available options below on how to restart the Retail Express Hardware Utility Service.

## Restarting using the Services App

1. Click the **Start** menu
2. Type **Services**
3. Open '**Services**'
  ![rtaImage - 2023-07-09T230644.014.png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000pphgpEAA.jpg)
4. Find '**Retail Express Hardware Service**'
5. Press '**Restart**'
  ![rtaImage - 2023-07-09T230649.205.png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000pphguEAA.jpg)
6. Log out of **POS**
7. Log into **POS**

## Restarting using the Hardware Utility Service

1. Click **Start** to open the Start Menu
2. Type **Retail**
3. Open **Retail Express Hardware Utility** app
  ![rtaImage - 2023-07-09T230654.456.png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000pphgzEAA.jpg)
4. Click the '**Service**' menu
5. Select '**Restart**'
  ![rtaImage - 2023-07-09T230658.736.png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000pphh4EAA.jpg)
6. **Log out** of POS
7. **Log into** POS
