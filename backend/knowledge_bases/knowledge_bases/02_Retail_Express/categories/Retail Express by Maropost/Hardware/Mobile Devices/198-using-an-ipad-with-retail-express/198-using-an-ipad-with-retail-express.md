# Using an iPad with Retail Express

You can leverage iPads as cost-effective POS terminals for **Retail Express**, enabling flexible retail operations with modern, portable hardware that customers and staff find intuitive. This helps optimize your retail operations by providing mobility for clienteling, reducing hardware costs compared to traditional POS terminals, and enabling modern retail experiences through familiar touch-screen interfaces.

Retail Express supports iPads, which means it's easy to access your system from wherever you are.

In fact, you can even use POS on the iPad, which means your staff can roam around the store to serve your customers, or you can add additional registers in times of peak periods (and includes the ability to use hardware connected to another PC to print receipts etc).

## []()Logging into Retail Express

To access Retail Express on your iPad you will need to do the following:

> **Note:** The steps below include information on setting your site as a bookmark on the iPad. This will ensure you can access your database easily in the future.

1. Open **Safari** on the iPad
2. Click the **URL bar** at the top of the screen
3. Type in the **address** of your database e.g. [https://training.retailexpress.com.au](https://training.retailexpress.com.au)
  ![rtaImage - 2023-07-10T042553.683.png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000ppj8QEAQ.jpg)
4. Press **Enter**. The login page will load
5. Click the **Share **icon
  ![rtaImage - 2023-07-10T042559.756.png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000ppj8VEAQ.jpg)
6. Click **Add to Favourites**
  ![rtaImage - 2023-07-10T042605.735.png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000ppj8aEAA.jpg)
7. Enter a **name** and click **Save**

> **Note:** Enter a name that's easily identifiable e.g. Back Office

8. Enter your **User ID** and **Password**
9. Click **Login**
10. If you are using Mobile Dashboards you will be presented with the Dashboard; scroll to the bottom, click **View Desktop Site** (bottom right-hand corner)** **and **OK**
11. The Back Office will be displayed
12. Click **POS **- a new tab will open with the login screen
13. Click the **Share** icon
14. Click **Add to Favourites**
15. Enter a **name** and click **Save**
  ![rtaImage - 2023-07-10T042611.439.png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000ppj8fEAA.jpg)

> **Note:** Enter a name that's easily identifiable e.g. POS

16. Enter your **User ID** and **Password**
17. Select an **Outlet** and **Register**

> **Note:** To print receipts etc you will need to install the [Hardware Certificate](https://galaxy.maropost.com/kb/articles/198-using-an-ipad-with-retail-express#hardware) on the iPad and select a Register that has been configured for hardware

You will now be logged into POS.

The next time you need to log in, access your Favourites by clicking the + icon (the list of links will be in the main section) or click the Bookmarks icon and select Favourites.

## []()Hardware Certificate Installation

To connect and print receipts using another computer's hardware you will need to install the Hardware Certificate on the iPad. These steps below should be followed directly on the iPad.

- **Download the Certificate file**

1. Log into the Back Office on the iPad
2. Navigate to **Help & Support > Knowledge Base **to open the Knowledge Base in a new tab
3. Type **iPad hardware** into the search bar
4. Select the article **Using an iPad with Retail Express** (this article)
5. Download the Hardware Certificate by clicking the link above
6. Click **Allow**
  ![rtaImage - 2023-07-10T042622.074.png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000ppj8uEAA.jpg)
7. Click **Close**
  ![rtaImage - 2023-07-10T042626.302.png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000ppi78EAA.jpg)
8. Navigate to **Settings > Profile Downloaded**
9. The profile **Retail Express Root Authority **should automatically open
10. Click **Install**
  ![rtaImage - 2023-07-10T042630.796.png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000ppiuPEAQ.jpg)
11. Enter your **Passcode**
12. Click **Install**
13. Click **Done**
14. Navigate to **Settings > General > About > Certificate Trust Settings**
15. Ensure the "Retail Express Root Authority" option has the **toggle on** for the setting "Enable full trust for Root Certificates"
  ![rtaImage - 2023-07-10T042635.504.png](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05T5g00000ppj8zEAA.jpg)

> **Note:** To view this profile in the future navigate to **General > Profile > Retail Express Root Authority.**

## []()**Assign Hardware to a new iPad Register**

To use hardware (receipt printer, integrated EFTPOS) with your iPad, you can select from either of the following options:

1. Select an existing Register currently configured for the hardware (follow the instructions above when logging in)
2. Reassign an existing Hardware Service using the iPad (including creating a new register)

Reassign hardware to a new iPad register:

To create a new Register for the iPad:

1. Using a PC, log into Retail Express
2. Navigate to Settings > POS Settings > Registers
3. Create a Register

> **Note:** Give the Register an identifiable name e.g. iPad

4. Navigate to Settings > POS Settings > Hardware Services
5. Click the pencil icon to edit the Hardware Service for the appropriate Outlet

> **Note:** If your Outlet isn't listed you will need to complete the Receipt Printer and Integrated EFTPOS installation for that Outlet

6. Tick the new Register
7. Click the Tick icon to save the changes

## []()Frequently Asked Questions

[Red "Error While Updating" message when opening POS](https://galaxy.maropost.com/kb/articles/198-using-an-ipad-with-retail-express#red-cache-error)

If you encounter an error when opening POS, you will need to clear the iPad's cache - the temporary storage space for Safari.

The cache allows Safari to open a previously visited web page without having to reload the page from scratch - it saves parts of it in the memory. If the page has been updated and the cache is invalidated, you may encounter this error.

Error While Updating

Either you are not connected to the internet or your browser has an issue. Please clear your browser cache (typically you can do this by pressing Ctrl+Shift_Del and select all options then click Remove) and try again.

To resolve the issue you will need to:

1. On the iPad click the Settings app
2. Click the Safari menu
3. Click Clear History and Website Data
4. Click Clear
