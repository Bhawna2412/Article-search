# Clear Browser Cache, History and Session Storage

You can resolve POS performance issues, stuck sales, and white screen errors by clearing your browser's cache, session storage, and browsing history, helping restore normal retail operations when technical glitches occur. Properly clearing browser data typically resolves most common POS display and functionality issues, minimizing transaction disruptions and ensuring smooth retail operations.

Understanding cache clearing procedures across Chrome, Edge, and Firefox helps retail teams quickly troubleshoot and resolve browser-related POS problems, maintaining consistent transaction processing capability across your retail locations.

If a sale is stuck, or you observe a plain white screen in POS and Ctrl F5 does not work, you may need to clear your Internet Browser's cache and browsing history.

## []()Google Chrome

[Clear Javascript](https://galaxy.maropost.com/kb/articles/233-clear-browser-cache-history-and-session-storage#chrome-javascript)

To clear the Javascript in Google Chrome:

1. Ensure you're on the POS page in Google Chrome
2. Press **F12** (this will open the Google Chrome Dev tools)
3. Right click on the **Reload** arrow next to the Address bar
4. Select **Empty Cache and Hard Reload**

> **Note:** If you can't see this menu it's likely the Developer Tools is not open. Press **F12** and try again.

5. The page will go white, press **Ctrl + F5 **to complete the refresh

[Clear Browser History](https://galaxy.maropost.com/kb/articles/233-clear-browser-cache-history-and-session-storage#chrome-browser-history)

To clear the Browser History in Chrome:

1. Click the menu icon in the top right-hand corner
2. Navigate to **History > History**
3. Click the **Clear browsing data **link on the left-hand side
4. Select **All time **from the Time range drop-down
5. Tick **Cached Images and Files, ****Cookies and other site data & Hosted App Data**
6. Click **Clear Data**

![image](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/image.05TJ100000Fr2q5MAB.jpg)
[Clear Session Storage](https://galaxy.maropost.com/kb/articles/233-clear-browser-cache-history-and-session-storage#chrome-session)

1. Click on the three dots in the top right-hand corner
2. Click **Settings**
3. Click **Privacy and Security**
4. Click **Site Settings**
5. Select **View permissions and data stored across sites**
6. Search for **Retail Express**
7. Click the **three dots**
8. Select **Clear Data**
