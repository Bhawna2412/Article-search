# Category & Content Export Wizard

You can export product categories and content data to make bulk updates efficiently or integrate with third-party systems, helping streamline catalog management operations. This export capability provides complete visibility into your category structure and content organization, enabling you to analyze catalog performance, prepare data for external systems, or make systematic improvements to your B2B product organization.

When you use the category content export wizard, you typically improve operational efficiency by accessing customizable data extracts tailored to your specific business needs. This helps ensure you can manage catalog data effectively, whether for marketing campaigns, inventory analysis, or third-party integrations, supporting your overall product catalog strategy with flexible data access.

Content and product categories can be exported into a CSV (comma separated values) file so that you can make changes in bulk or import into a third party system. You can also export content lists to make changes, and then import back into Neto. There's 2 ways to export your content:

> **Note:** Neto partners can set this up for you. [Request a data service job](https://www.netohq.com/job-request-portal) for a quote.

## Simple Export

The simple export creates a CSV file of your content in Neto's standard format. It can't be customised as much as the complex export, and filters are limited. Third party applications will likely have a specific format required in which case the complex export is better suited.

To use the simple export:

1.   - Products > Product categories
  - Products > Brands
  - Webstore > Blog post
  - Webstore > Buying guide
  - Webstore > Web page

  In your Neto control panel, navigate to your content. You can find content in several different places in your control panel:
2. Click the **Export** button at the top of the page.
  **Please Note**: Filters applied to the content list will not be carried over to the export results.
3. Click the **Preview** button.
4. Clear the checkboxes for fields you don't want exported, and click the **Export** button.
5. The CSV file of your content will be created. Click the **Download** button when it appears, to save the file to your computer.

## Complex Export

The complex export wizard gives you a lot more control over the fields that are exported, filters, and allows you to set up schedules to export on a regular basis. To use the complex export wizard:

1. In your Neto control panel navigate to **Settings & tools** > **Export data**.
2. Select the **Product Categories** radio button.
3. Click the **Perform complex export** button and choose the **Start Data Export Wizard** option.
4. Enter a name for your file and click the **Continue to Next Step** button.
5. You now need to choose which content fields you want to export. Click the **Add New Field To File** button, and choose the fields from the drop down menus.
  You need to select a field type first (e.g. content, content path, related content table) and then choose the field name.

> **Note:** We recommend including the **Content** > **ID** and **Content Name** fields to help you identify the content exported.

6. Repeat the previous step and add all the fields you need in your file. When complete, click **Continue to Next Step**.

> **Note:** You can change the column header for each field on the right hand side of the page. This is useful if you export to a third party system that has specific field name requirements.

7. You can now determine which content is included in the file by selecting filters.
  For example, change the content type drop down menu to **Brand** to export your product brands.

  When you've finished selecting your filters click the **Continue to Next Step** button.
8. In the **Send Export File To** field, select how you want to receive your content export file.

> **Note:** Choose Local URL to save the file in your site assets, so it can be downloaded when ready. You'll be shown a URL where it can be downloaded from.

  Make sure you add any required details (e.g. email address, FTP username/password) depending on your selection.
9. Select the checkbox if you want to save the export settings (name, fields, filters etc) so that you can run the export again later. If you do, enter a template name and optionally select an automated export schedule.
10. Click the **Export Now** button.

The export process will be queued to run, create the file, and deliver it to your chosen location.

> **Note:** Click on the export process ID to view a page with the status of your export. Click the **Run Now** button on this page to start the process immediately.
