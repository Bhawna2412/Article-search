# Control Panel Management

## Control Panel Navigation

The Neto control panel is divided in the following way:

![Image](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/ControlPanelManagement1.05T5g00000gQ9eUEAS.png)

| Number | Name | Definition |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Tool bar | Contains a variety of system related information and tools including:  - **Search** bar - Informational messages and notifications (e.g. number of days remaining on a trials) - User Profile information (Change Password, Manage Subscription, Refer a Friend, View Webstore, Sign Out - **Help** button (link to Neto Support Hub). |
| 2 | Navigation pane | Navigation tool for locating pages within a webstore. 			Selecting an option from the **Navigation** pane may reveal an additional list of options from where a page can be selected and displayed. |
| 3 | Page | Displays information based on navigation from the **Navigation** pane. |
| 4 | Menu | Navigation tool for locating sections within a page. 			Selecting an option from a menu will move the page to the selected section. |
| 5 | Section | Information separated or divided within a page. |

## System Email Addresses

> **Note:** Every system email template can be customised in the email templates section of your control panel, including customising the From, To and BCC email addresses. If these have been customised, they will overwrite the below settings.

To set your system email addresses:

1. In your Neto control panel navigate to **Settings & tools** > **Business details**.
2. Enter your system email address and/or BCC email address in the fields provided.

## Dispute Emails

By default, when a customer lodges a dispute via the Customer Resolution centre and email is sent to the System e-mail address. This can be overridden by enabling the following options:

| Configuration | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Turn on dispute email address | Dispute email address is the default address that Dispute messages go to. |
| E-mail for disputes to be sent | Customer dispute emails will be sent to this address. |

1. In your Neto control panel navigate to **Settings & tools** > **All settings & tools**.
2. Select **Advanced Configuration**.
3. In the Name field, search for **USE_DISPUTE_EMAIL** and select it.
4. Change the **Value** to **Yes**. Click **Save Changes** and then click **Back to configuration Manager.**
  ![Image](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/ControlPanelManagement5.05T5g00000gQ9hOEAS.png)
5. In the Name field, search for **DISPUTE_EMAIL** and select it.
6. Change the value to the email address you would like your dispute notifications to be sent to. Click **Save Changes**
  ![Image](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/ControlPanelManagement6.05T5g00000gQ9jFEAS.png)

## Logos

![image](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/ControlPanelManagement7.05T5g00000gQ9kmEAC.png)
You business logo is one of, if not **the** most important aspect of your front end design. Without an identifying logo it's difficult for your customers to remember your brand, thus not coming back to buy more!

**A logo can provide a really quick insight into the type of brand you are, what you sell and to who.**

A good logo is distinctive, appropriate, practical, graphic and simple in form. A logo should be able to be printed at any size and, in most cases, be effective without colour. A great logo essentially boils down to two things: great concept and great execution.

Depending on the type of business you are, and the stage your business is at, launching your Neto webstore alongside a new or reviewed logo is an effective way to position yourself in your niche market.

When creating or reviewing your logo follow a process that ensures the final design meets the needs of your business. Research your industry and compare your logo with your competitors. Conceptualise your logo and develop it over time. Your logo is important and should take time.

When sitting down to design your logo, or to pass your ideas on to a designer, it's handy to keep the following core principles in mind:

1. **Simple** - Simplicity makes a logo design easily recognisable, versatile and memorable.
2. **Memorable** - Simplicity leads into memorability. A logo design, when simple, is easy to remember.
3. **Timeless** - A logo should still be effective and relevant 10, 20 or 30 years down the road.
4. **Versatile** - Your logo should be presentable in one colour or various colours & still recognisable when small or large.
5. **Appropriate** - How you present your logo should be appropriate for your intended audience. Child-like font style might be okay for a child clothing store or a toy store , but not for a supplement or camping store.

### Recommended Website Logo Sizes

Most theme logos are responsive and will scale the logo between 350px and 450px. We recommend uploading a logo with a width of 350px. The height will then depend on how square you want the logo to display.

## Customise Your Control Panel

### Control Panel Theme

If you have multiple Neto accounts it's recommended you change the theme of your control panel. This helps you identify which account you are logged into easily. To change your control panel theme:

1. In your Neto control panel navigate to the **Settings & Tools** > **All Settings & Tools**.
2. Click the **Control Panel** link in the menu.
3. Click the **Control Panel Settings** link.
  The **Control Panel Branding** section contains fields for changing the theme and logo.
4. Click the **Cpanel theme** dropdown menu and choose a new colour theme.
  ![Image](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/ControlPanelManagement8.05T5g00000gQ9krEAC.png)
5. Try different themes to see which one you like best.
6. Scroll to the bottom of the page and click the **Save Changes** button.

### Control Panel Logo

The logo that displays in your control panel in the bottom right corner can be updated to match your business:

1. In your Neto control panel navigate to the **Settings & Tools** > **All Settings & Tools**.
2. Click the **Control Panel** link in the menu.
3. Click the **Control Panel Settings** link.
4. Click the **Choose file** button within the **cPanel Logo** field.
5. Navigate to the relevant logo and click the **Open** button.
6. Scroll to the bottom of the page and click the **Save Changes** button.
