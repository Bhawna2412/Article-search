# Drop Shipping Overview

You can implement drop shipping to expand your product catalog without holding physical inventory, enabling you to offer a broader range of products to B2B customers while reducing warehouse and fulfillment costs. This helps streamline order fulfillment by automating the routing of orders to suppliers who ship directly to your customers, reducing manual order processing and improving operational efficiency.

Properly configured drop shipping typically improves scalability by supporting multiple suppliers per order, automated stock level synchronization, and custom order file formats for each supplier. When you enable drop shipping operations, you can manage supplier relationships efficiently while maintaining brand consistency in customer communications, allowing you to grow your B2B product offerings without proportional increases in warehousing or fulfillment infrastructure.

## Workflow

The diagram below displays how information moves between the supplier, seller and customer. This is a common workflow, but drop shipping can be set up and run a number of ways depending on the supplier or seller's systems.

![image2](https://us.v-cdn.net/6038474/uploads/attachments/migrated/DropShippingOverview2.05T5g00000v3HhkEAE.png)

1. The supplier sends the product lists and stock levels to the seller to display on their website or online marketplace (e.g. eBay, Catch, Amazon) accounts. Using Neto, this data can be automated to send regularly to add new products and keep available stock up to date.
2. The seller publishes the products they want to sell on their website or online marketplace where customers can view them.
3. Customer makes a purchase and the order is created for the seller.
4. The seller sends the order to the supplier for fulfilment. Using Neto, sales orders can be exported automatically on a regular basis and created in the supplier's control panel.
5. The seller processes the order and dispatches the products directly to the customer. The order status is updated, and this information is sent to the seller to update the orders in their system. Using Neto, the dispatched status and tracking information can be sent automatically and updated in the seller's control panel automatically.
6. The supplier emails a dispatch notification and tracking number to the customer when the order is processed. Using Neto, the email can mimic the seller's brand and styling in the email to make it look like the order has come directly from where they purchased the product.

## Set up Drop Shipping

How you set up drop shipping depends on a number of factors:

- Are you the drop ship supplier or seller?
- What system is the other party using?
- How does it connect to Neto?
- How will data be transferred (file vs. API)?

The below set up guides assume you are using CSV data feeds to transfer products, stock level, and order information. CSV is a common format across many different systems due to its ease of use and reliability.
