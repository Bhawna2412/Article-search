# The ABC of Improving Email Deliverability

You can apply systematic A/B testing, bounce rate monitoring, and list cleansing practices to significantly improve your email deliverability performance and maximize campaign effectiveness. These foundational strategies help ensure your marketing messages reach intended recipients while building stronger sender reputation over time.

Implementing structured email optimization practices typically leads to better engagement rates and inbox placement. When properly maintained, clean lists and data-driven testing can enhance your email marketing ROI by reducing waste and improving subscriber interaction with your campaigns.

Imagine for a second you were put into a Groundhog Day-type loop. Every morning, you'd re-live the same day, and you'd be able to optimize a single email, a single blog, or an ad. Imagine the advancement you'd make.

Until that actually happens, it's best to have a simple trick to keep in mind when optimizing your emails. We call this the A, B, C of email deliverability. A is for A/B split testing, B is for Bounce-rate and C is for Cleanliness - Email List. Of course, there are many more tricks and tips for optimizing emails, but these are good to get started with:

### A is for A/B split testing

A/B Split testing is one of the fastest ways to improve email deliverability.

A/B split testing is a method that you can use to compare two (or more) different emails to see which one performs better. The reason A/B split testing is so effective is that you get real data, such as open and click rates, to help you decide on which email to send.

Having A/B split testing implemented into your email marketing program is a surefire way to increase the engagement rate for your emails. This is good practice to have even for beginner email marketers.

### B is for Bounce rate

Bounce rate is one of the [**10 essential KPIs**](https://galaxy.maropost.com/home/leaving?allowTrusted=1&target=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.maropost.com%2Fblog%2F10-essential-email-marketing-metrics-and-kpis%2F) to keep in mind when optimizing your email marketing program.

There are two types of bounce rates to watch out for. The first is a soft bounce that usually happens when an inbox is full, or the person is out of office, or something less critical.

A hard bounce occurs when the email address is invalid or belongs to the wrong person. If the hard bounce rate starts to creep up, time to move on to the next section - list cleanliness. Cleaning up your list is one way to ensure your bounce rate remains low.  However, to ensure your list remains clean, you should look to implement proper opt-in permissions.

Depending on the industry, the average hard bounce rate fluctuates around 2%. If your hard bounce rate moves beyond, you'll want to take a look at the quality of emails in your list.

### C is for Cleanliness

The next step in optimizing email deliverability is keeping your list clean. If you want to continue growing the engagement rates for the emails you send, you'll want to invest in some list cleaning.

If it's been a while since your emails have been cleaned, you're most likely going to see a high bounce rate. There are many reasons for this, such as not having the correct email address. Cleaning up your list is a simple way to keep your hard bounces low and ensure your emails are being delivered and read.

There you have it, those are our ABCs for optimizing email deliverability. Of course, there are many ways to improve your email deliverability, but this is a good start.
